## What This IS

The operating system for eating well on GLP-1s.

MemoryAisle is the only app that treats GLP-1 nutrition as a body-composition problem, not a weight-loss problem. The core insight: when appetite is suppressed, every bite must be engineered to preserve lean mass, manage symptoms, and fuel training. 39% of weight lost on GLP-1s is lean mass (STEP 1 trial). That is the problem we solve.

The app adapts meals, portions, grocery lists, and daily guidance based on: appetite suppression level, symptom state (nausea, food aversion, constipation), protein shortfall risk, training day vs rest day, medication phase (injection day, peak suppression, appetite return), and maintenance/taper phase.

### Upcoming (track for future support)
| Medication | Status | Notes |
|------------|--------|-------|
| Oral tirzepatide | Phase 2/3 trials | Mounjaro/Zepbound in pill form. Not yet approved. |
| Retatrutide | Phase 3 | Triple agonist (GLP-1/GIP/glucagon). 24% avg weight loss in trials. |

### Why Modality Matters for Nutrition

**Injectable users:** Weekly cycle creates predictable appetite pattern. Days 1-2 post-injection: lowest appetite, highest nausea risk. Days 5-7: appetite returns. Meal plans should follow this wave.

**Oral with fasting (Wegovy pill, Rybelsus, Ozempic tablets):** Must take on empty stomach and wait 30 minutes before eating. This changes breakfast planning entirely. GI side effects may be more acute since the drug hits the stomach directly. Meal timing guidance is critical.

**Oral without fasting (Foundayo):** No food restrictions. Can take with any meal. This is the simplest modality for nutrition planning. The app should recognize this and not burden these users with unnecessary fasting guidance.

**The app must ask which medication AND which form (injection vs pill) during onboarding, and adjust all meal timing, portion guidance, and symptom predictions accordingly.**

Users can switch modes at any time. Mira may suggest a mode change based on data (e.g., "You've been logging nausea 4 out of 7 days. Want me to switch to Sensitive Stomach mode?").

## The Moat: Adaptive Decision Engine

The moat is not recipes and not tracking. The moat is a decision engine that adapts to:

```
Inputs:
  - Appetite suppression level (user-reported or inferred from meal logging)
  - Symptom state (nausea, food aversion, constipation, bloating)
  - Protein shortfall risk (grams behind target at current meal)
  - Training day vs rest day (connected calendar or manual input)
  - Medication phase (days since injection, or daily pill timing)
  - Medication modality (injectable, oral with fasting, oral without fasting)
  - Maintenance/taper phase (dose changes, coming off)
  - Pantry state (what user has at home via barcode scan or manual input)
  - Time of day + cooking capacity ("I have 10 minutes" vs "I can cook tonight")
  - GI tolerance history (which foods triggered symptoms before)

Outputs:
  - Today's meal plan (adjusted to all inputs above)
  - Adjusted portion sizes (smaller on high-nausea days, larger on appetite-return days)
  - Grocery list (only what's needed, filtered for GI tolerance)
  - In-store barcode verdict ("Good choice: 24g protein, nausea-safe" or "Skip: high fat, slow gastric emptying")
  - Protein recovery suggestions ("You're 22g behind. Greek yogurt + hemp seeds closes the gap in one snack.")
  - Training-day fuel adjustments (pre-workout carbs, post-workout protein)
  - Hydration reminders (GLP-1s suppress thirst response)
  - Provider report data (weekly PDF: protein compliance, weight trend, symptoms, adherence)
```

That decision engine is much harder to copy than content. The more the system turns those inputs into meal choices, grocery changes, and recovery-aware guidance, the more defensible it becomes.

## Mira: The AI Engine

Mira is not a chatbot bolted onto a dashboard. Mira IS the product. The UI is what Mira builds around you.

### Mira's Role
- Onboarding: introduces herself, asks setup questions, builds your personalized interface
- Daily guidance: proactive suggestions based on your state today
- Meal planning: generates meals adapted to appetite, symptoms, training, pantry
- In-store companion: interprets barcode scans in context of your protocol
- Recovery coach: detects protein shortfalls and suggests specific fixes
- Mode transitions: suggests switching modes based on your data patterns

### Mira's Guardrails
- Never diagnoses medical conditions
- Never recommends stopping or changing medication
- Always defers to "talk to your prescriber" for medical questions
- Grounded in published clinical guidance (STEP 1, OASIS 4, Mayo Clinic, Cleveland Clinic)
- Acknowledges uncertainty explicitly
- Medical disclaimer on all nutrition advice
- Rate-limited: 10 messages/day (free), unlimited (Pro)

### Mira's Context Window (per request)
```
- User profile (medication, dose, modality, injection day, dietary restrictions)
- Current mode (Everyday, Sensitive Stomach, Muscle Preservation, etc.)
- Last 7 days of meal logs (protein, calories, hydration)
- Last 7 days of symptom logs
- Training schedule (this week)
- Current protein deficit/surplus
- GI tolerance history (foods that triggered symptoms)
- Pantry state (if available)
- Time of day
```

## Onboarding Flow (Mira-First)

The app opens to Mira. No dashboard. No signup form. Just Mira.

### Screen 1: Introduction
Mira's waveform (5 white bars + bright white star, no circle, no container) centered on screen, animating as she speaks. Voice-activated from first launch. Mic button at bottom. User can speak or tap.
"Hello. I'm Mira. Your personal nutrition companion for this journey."
CTA: "Let's get started"
Subtitle below mic: "Tap to speak, or just listen"

### Screen 2: Are you on GLP-1 medication?
Options:
- Yes, I'm on a GLP-1 medication
- No, I just want smarter nutrition
(If no, skip to Screen 5: Goals. The app works for everyone. GLP-1 is the wedge, not the wall.)

### Screen 3: Medication + Modality
"Which medication are you on?"
- Ozempic (injection)
- Wegovy (injection)
- Wegovy pill (oral, daily)
- Mounjaro (injection)
- Zepbound (injection)
- Foundayo (oral, daily, no fasting)
- Rybelsus / Ozempic tablets (oral, daily)
- Compounded semaglutide
- Compounded tirzepatide
- Other

### Screen 4: Dose + Timing
Dose picker (medication-specific dose options)
For injectable: injection day selector (M/T/W/T/F/S/S)
For oral with fasting: "What time do you take your pill?" + "Mira will plan breakfast around your 30-minute window"
For Foundayo (oral, no fasting): "No timing restrictions. I'll plan meals for maximum absorption and comfort."
Mira explains: "I'll adjust your meals based on where you are in your cycle."

### Screen 5: Biggest Worry (multi-select)
"What worries you most right now?"
- Losing muscle, not just fat
- Nausea and food aversions
- Not getting enough protein
- Not knowing what to buy at the store
- Regaining weight if I stop
- Hair loss or nutrient gaps
- Low energy for workouts

### Screen 6: Training
"Do you train or exercise regularly?"
- Yes, I lift weights (-> unlocks Muscle Preservation / Training Performance modes)
- Yes, cardio/general fitness
- Sometimes
- Not currently

### Screen 7: Dietary Restrictions
Quick multi-select: Vegetarian, Vegan, Gluten-free, Dairy-free, Halal, Kosher, Nut allergy, None

### Screen 8: Mira Ready
Summary card of everything. Mira reads it back (voice enabled).
"I've set everything up. I'll plan your meals around your [medication] cycle and make sure every bite counts."
CTA: "Take me home"

The home screen that loads is now personalized. A Muscle Preservation user sees protein front and center. A Sensitive Stomach user sees nausea-safe meal suggestions and symptom logging. Mira built it for them.
